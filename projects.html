Derelict Star is my personal vision of what a Metroid game could be. It takes most of the mechanics of the classic Metroid games, adds in some melee combat reminiscent of Castlevania, and throws in some of my favorite content from various other games (Flight from Ranger-x, vehicular action scenes from Earthworm Jim, and drones similar to various space-shooters). It's hard to think of truly original content for a 2D action platformer at this day and age, but by combining my favorites and tweaking them to work cohesively I think the overall effect will be quite rewarding in its own rite.

Pet's Amok (Garden of Shinkai) was the first Java project that I went overboard on. It's a (Java) console game that at this time only interacts through text, but could be made into a full-fledged app similar to some Gocha games. The player begins with a semi-random set of Spirits with which they are tasked to heal and nurture. At any time they can summon new spirits into their garden, or release a spirit out into the great beyond. The state of a spirit being released will have an impact on your overall Karma, so you are encouraged to treat the spirits well. Each spirit has a set of attributes that is determined by its nature, and must be dealt with in an essentially unique way.
